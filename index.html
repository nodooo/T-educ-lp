The Perfect Study Space to Invoke Success

Preparing yourself for an online trading lesson might be even harder than going to lectures. For the latter, all you need to do is to get up and show up. For the former, you need to be in the right mental state to retain the information being shared on your screen.
Live instructional classes and real-time market updates will require your full attention.

Sure, focusing is not easy in either case. The difference is that during lectures, you have no other option but to pay attention. As for online trading lessons, there can be so many distractions. The well-known example of getting up and looking into the fridge 5 times during a lesson, is just one of them.

    Here is a list of possible distractions:

  • Noise - The most common distraction you will face is noise. Shockingly enough, silence could be the noise that your mind can’t comprehend. Though most of the websites and researches might advise you to turn off all music and sounds, it might be smarter to actually keep some kind of noise in the background, such as ambient music. Experiment and see which type of noise suits your course style the best. Maybe for the ‘Basics on Trading Instruments’ lesson, white noise will be best. And maybe for the ‘Trading Terminologies’ lesson, you'll need complete silence. You never know.

  • Lighting - Is it too dim? Too bright? Does it give you headaches? Can you see clearly? There are many questions that follow the subject of optimized study light, but what works best is debatable. Personally, desk lamps work best for my productivity. Maybe you prefer lighthouse lighting or dim lighting. Over the years, researchers have suggested using natural light as it reduces stress. Thus improving mood and morale, aiding focus, and concentration. MetaTrader lessons might require natural light for you, but the final exam may go much better with a desk lamp. So, make sure to test what feels right for you.

  • Phone - You can’t be surprised that this is on the list. One of the leading distractions in the world are mobile phones. Whether it’s social media, games, or texting, it surely gets in the way. Make sure to keep your phone on Do Not Disturb, or at least silent mode, to aid your studies at home. The best way is to use your phone for select periods of time - for example, no phone for 30 minutes, then phone 5 minutes, and so on.

    • Oh, and if you are using your mobile phone for the online trading course - make sure it's fully charged and don't forget to test the screen brightness!

  • Hunger - Did you eat too much? Not enough? Unhealthy? All of these factors contribute to focus loss. A healthy brain will produce a healthy amount of energy which you will use for focus. Eating too much will make you lazy, while not enough food can make you scatter-brained. At the same time, eating unhealthy foods will not provide your body with the nutrients it needs to produce energy.

  • Temperature - If you are too cold, you will get the shivers, which will counteract with your concentration. If you are too warm, there is a chance you will get too comfortable. Researchers advise setting the room to the average temperature, between 20-22 celsius or 68-72 Fahrenheit. Enough research has been done to prove that these temperatures create the most focus. But, make sure to experiment and see what your personal preference is. Remember, every lesson requires your maximum focus. Therefore, if it's a Fibonacci Trading lesson or a live Consulting Session, make sure you optimize the settings.

  • Discomfort - Is your chair comfortable? Is your desk up too high? There are actually certain ways to build your work desk, and we made it easy for you to follow with this infographic attached. Want to read an E-Book? Maybe this one will be better on the sofa.

  • Clutter - Get all the excess decorations and distractions off of your desk. Keep only 3 of the most important ones - whatever motivates you. Whether it’s a picture of your car, newborn, or your parents, stick to the essentials. Do you want a small bobble-head? Sure, but only if it will remind you what you are learning here. All of the clutter on your desk should be designed to remind you that your hard work is appreciated, and will pay off. It can even be a poster of a Trading Plan or the Economic Calendar if you want the motivation straight in your face.

  • Disorganization - Don’t just study at any time of the day. Create a schedule and follow through. There is no actual best time of the day to study. Some people prefer early mornings, while others prefer midnight. Experiment and see when you get the most focus and energy, and use it to your advantage. If you don’t have the luxury to pick and choose, distribute your time evenly and prioritize.

  • Colors - There is a reason you don’t see bright red offices or schools. They distract you, scattering your focus all over those bright colors. Instead, try to sit near a neutral colored wall. White, beige, grey even, but don’t make it too boring for yourself. Facing a blank wall will only make your mind wander. Try to face your desk towards a free space, so your mind can relax.

  • Footwear - Researchers say that keeping your feet professional will give you a boost of focus. What does that mean? That means that wearing shoes - any kind of outerwear shoes - will help you concentrate. Your mind will associate the shoes with being outside. So, rather than being extra cozy in your bunny slippers, put on your sneakers, and get to work.

    • We hope these tips will help you get organized for your next online trading course. Through trial and error, you will learn what works for you and what does not. Make sure to change your environment as much as you need until you get it perfect.

    Adjusting these 10 main distractions will help you create the perfect study space to Invoke your Success.
    Good luck!
